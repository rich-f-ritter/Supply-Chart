Within 5 miles of Canyon Ridge (2552 E Gowen Rd, SE Boise), the dominant land-use fact is the Boise Airport / Gowen Field. About 18,700 of the ~50,200 acres in the radius (37%) lie in the Airport Influence Area overlay, and roughly 9,400 acres of that — the Zone B (4,460 ac) and Zone C (4,961 ac) sub-areas — are off-limits to NEW residential under City/County code (Canyon Ridge itself plots inside Zone B). That single fact reshapes the supply picture: most vacant, MF-zoned land closest to the subject (the MX-2 parcels along Federal Way, Eisenman, Airport Way and the Columbia town-center node) cannot add competing apartments and is instead building out as airport-adjacent industrial/logistics (Red River, Boyer/Boise Gateway, Adler, Sudler, Pleasant Valley) and, to the east, the ~900-acre Micron campus expansion ($50B, two new fabs). Genuinely by-right multifamily-developable vacant land in the radius is scarce — only ~248 acres of vacant land carries High (by-right MF) zoning, and most of it is either inside the airport no-residential zones or is small infill. The real competitive apartment-supply threat therefore sits OUTSIDE the airport cone and farther from the subject: a 62-ac R-3 tract on W Victory Rd (~3.5 mi), the MX-3/MX-1 mixed-use tracts around Parkcenter/the Boise River (~2–4 mi), and scattered R-2/R-3 infill. The biggest housing pipelines (East Hollow ~7,000 homes near Micron, CBH 'Locale' ~2,000, Murio Farms ~3,000) are master-planned single-family on the rural-urban frontier, gated by the Lake Hazel Rd extension and secondary-access/fire constraints — long-horizon, mostly for-sale product, not near-term apartment competition. Net: the airport is effectively a supply moat on the subject's airport-facing sides; underwrite competition from the non-airport SE/Parkcenter submarkets, not from the vacant MX land next door.
